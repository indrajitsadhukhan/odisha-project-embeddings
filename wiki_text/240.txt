Temple in Odisha, India


Rameshwar DeulaReligionAffiliationHinduismDistrictBhubaneswarDeityRameshwar DeulaLocationLocationBhubaneswarStateOdishaCountryIndiaGeographic coordinates20°15′12.49″N 85°50′9.30″E﻿ / ﻿20.2534694°N 85.8359167°E﻿ / 20.2534694; 85.8359167Coordinates: 20°15′12.49″N 85°50′9.30″E﻿ / ﻿20.2534694°N 85.8359167°E﻿ / 20.2534694; 85.8359167
The Rameshwar temple is very old temple in Bhubaneswar, Odisha, India, and known as the Mausi Maa temple of Lingaraj Temple. It is located from 2 km distance from Lingaraj shrine.


Legend[edit]
Legend goes as when Rama was returning from Lanka after victory over Ravana, Goddess Sita asked to worship Shiva here. So Ramachandra built a Linga for that purpose.[citation needed]
Traditionally during Ashokashtami, which falls one day before to Rama Navami  in Chaitra Lord Lingaraja comes to this temple by a large chariot called Rukuna Rath and stays for four days. Historically the temple dates back to 9th century.
